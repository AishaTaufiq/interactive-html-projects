What is a Particle?
A particle is a candidate solution — a point in the search space. Each glowing firefly on screen represents one particle exploring where the optimum might be. It has a position (where it is) and a velocity (how fast & which direction it moves).
What is Velocity?
Velocity is the direction and speed of a particle's movement. Each iteration, velocity is adjusted based on three forces: inertia (keep moving as before), cognitive pull (drift toward own best), and social pull (drift toward swarm's best). The green arrow shows each particle's velocity.
What is Personal Best (pBest)?
pBest is the best position this particle has ever visited. Shown as a purple ghost dot, it acts as the particle's own memory — always pulling it back toward its best discovered location.
What is Global Best (gBest)?
gBest is the best solution the entire swarm has ever found. Shown as a blazing gold star ★, it acts as the shared knowledge that attracts all particles. Once any particle finds a better solution, gBest is updated and the whole swarm shifts toward it.
Exploration vs Exploitation
Early on, particles explore widely (high randomness, spread out). Over time they exploit — narrowing in on promising regions. This balance is controlled by the inertia weight ω, which decreases over iterations from 0.9 → 0.4.
