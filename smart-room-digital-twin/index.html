About Digital Twins
What is a Digital Twin? ▾
A digital twin is a virtual replica of a physical system, updated in real time using sensor data. It mirrors the real world digitally, allowing monitoring, simulation, and control without physically interacting with the system.
How are systems represented? ▾
Physical properties like temperature, humidity, and energy are captured by IoT sensors and streamed to the digital model. The virtual room here simulates this: sliders represent sensor inputs, and the room reacts accordingly.
Real-world Applications ▾
Digital twins power smart buildings, manufacturing, healthcare, aerospace, and city planning. NASA used them for Apollo 13. Modern factories use them to predict machine failures before they occur.
